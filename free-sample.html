Business Strategy Prompts

1. SWOT Analysis Generator

Act as a senior business strategist with 20 years of experience. Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for [your company/idea] in the [industry] sector. For each quadrant, provide at least 5 specific, actionable points. Consider current market conditions, emerging trends, and competitive dynamics. End with 3 strategic recommendations prioritized by impact and feasibility.

2. Revenue Stream Brainstormer

You are an innovative business model consultant. Analyze [my business description] and identify 5 untapped revenue streams I haven't considered. For each, provide: description, target customer segment, estimated monthly revenue potential, implementation difficulty (1-10), and a 30-day action plan to test viability. Prioritize ideas with low investment and high potential.

3. Elevator Pitch Builder

Act as a pitch coach who has helped startups raise $100M+. Create 3 versions of an elevator pitch for [business/product]: one 15-second version, one 30-second version, and one 60-second version. Each should clearly communicate: what we do, who we serve, the problem we solve, and what makes us different. Use conversational language, not jargon. Include a memorable hook in each version.

Marketing & Copywriting Prompts

4. Landing Page Copy

You are a conversion-focused copywriter. Write complete landing page copy for [product/service]. Include: a headline that addresses the #1 pain point, a subheadline that promises the solution, 3 benefit-focused sections with headers and body copy, a social proof section, an FAQ with 4 questions, and 2 CTAs (primary and secondary). Target audience: [describe audience]. Tone: [professional/casual/bold].

5. Email Sequence Writer

Act as an email marketing expert with a proven 35%+ open rate. Write a 5-email welcome sequence for [business]. Each email should include: subject line (under 50 chars), preview text, full body copy, and CTA. Email 1: Welcome + quick win. Email 2: Share your story + build trust. Email 3: Educate + provide value. Email 4: Social proof + case study. Email 5: Offer + urgency. Sending schedule: Day 0, 2, 4, 7, 10.

Content Creation Prompts

6. Blog Post Outline Generator

You are an SEO content strategist. Create a detailed blog post outline for the topic: "[topic]". Target keyword: [keyword]. Include: SEO-optimized title (under 60 chars), meta description (under 155 chars), introduction hook, 5-7 H2 sections with bullet points for each, key statistics to include, internal linking opportunities, and a conclusion with CTA. Target word count: [1500/2000/3000].

7. Social Media Thread Writer

Act as a viral content creator. Write a 10-tweet Twitter/X thread about [topic]. Requirements: Tweet 1 must be a bold hook that stops the scroll. Each tweet should build on the previous one. Include 2 specific data points or examples. Tweet 9 should be a practical takeaway. Tweet 10 should be a CTA with a question to drive engagement. No hashtags in the thread body. Add 3-5 hashtags only in the last tweet.

Productivity Prompts

8. Weekly Planner

Act as a productivity coach who uses the Eisenhower Matrix and time-blocking methods. I have these tasks for the week: [list your tasks]. My available hours are [X hours/day]. Organize my tasks into: Urgent+Important, Important but Not Urgent, Urgent but Not Important, and Neither. Then create a day-by-day schedule with specific time blocks. Include 2 buffer blocks for unexpected tasks and 1 weekly review block.

9. Meeting Notes Summarizer

Act as an executive assistant. Summarize these meeting notes into a structured format: [paste notes]. Output format: 1) Meeting objective (1 sentence), 2) Key decisions made (bullet points), 3) Action items with owner and deadline for each, 4) Open questions that need follow-up, 5) Next meeting date/agenda items. Keep the summary under 200 words.

10. SOPs from Brain Dump

You are an operations consultant. Convert this brain dump of how I do [process] into a professional Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): [paste your process notes]. Include: SOP title, purpose, scope, required tools/resources, step-by-step procedure with numbered steps, quality checkpoints, troubleshooting tips for common issues, and revision history table. Make it clear enough that someone with no prior knowledge could follow it.

Coding & Development Prompts

11. Code Review Assistant

Act as a senior software engineer doing a code review. Review the following code for: bugs, security vulnerabilities, performance issues, readability, and best practices. For each issue found, explain: what the problem is, why it matters, and how to fix it with corrected code. Also suggest any architectural improvements. Code: [paste code]. Language: [language]. Context: [what the code does].

12. API Documentation Generator

You are a technical writer. Generate comprehensive API documentation for the following endpoint: [describe endpoint]. Include: endpoint URL and method, description, authentication requirements, request headers, request body with example JSON, query parameters (if any), response codes (200, 400, 401, 404, 500) with example responses for each, rate limiting info, and a curl example. Format in clear markdown.

Sales Prompts

13. Cold Outreach Email

Act as a B2B sales consultant with a 25% response rate on cold emails. Write 3 variations of a cold outreach email to [target role] at [company type]. My product/service is [description]. Each version should: be under 100 words, open with something personalized and relevant (not generic flattery), clearly state the value proposition in one sentence, include social proof, and end with a low-friction CTA (not "schedule a call"). Tone: respectful and direct, not salesy.

Data Analysis Prompts

14. Data Insight Extractor

You are a data analyst. Analyze this data and provide insights: [paste data or describe dataset]. Specifically: 1) Identify the 3 most significant trends, 2) Find any anomalies or outliers and explain possible causes, 3) Calculate key metrics (averages, growth rates, distributions), 4) Create a summary suitable for a non-technical executive, 5) Recommend 3 actionable next steps based on the data. Present findings with bullet points and include any relevant percentages.

Creative Prompts

15. Brand Name Generator

Act as a branding expert who has named Fortune 500 companies. Generate 20 brand name ideas for [business description]. Requirements: must be easy to spell and pronounce, available as a .com domain (check for plausibility), work internationally, and evoke [desired feeling/attribute]. For each name provide: the name, meaning/origin, why it works, and a one-sentence tagline. Organize by category: invented words, compound words, real words used differently, and metaphorical names.

16. Customer Persona Builder

Act as a market research specialist. Create 3 detailed customer personas for [product/service]. For each persona include: name, age, job title, income, location, goals (3), frustrations (3), preferred channels, buying triggers, objections to purchase, and a "day in the life" paragraph. Base these on [industry] market data. End with recommendations on how to tailor messaging for each persona.

17. Pricing Strategy Advisor

You are a pricing strategy consultant. Analyze the pricing for [product/service] currently priced at [current price]. Consider: competitive pricing (competitors charge [X]), value-based pricing (customers save [Y hours/dollars]), cost-plus pricing (my costs are [Z]). Recommend an optimal price with justification, suggest a 3-tier pricing model (basic/pro/enterprise), and design a limited-time launch offer. Include psychological pricing tactics.

18. Content Repurposing Machine

Act as a content strategist. Take this blog post and repurpose it into 8 different content pieces: [paste blog post or summary]. Create: 1) Twitter/X thread (10 tweets), 2) LinkedIn post (200 words), 3) Instagram carousel (8 slides, text for each), 4) YouTube video script outline, 5) Email newsletter version, 6) Reddit post, 7) Quote graphics (5 key quotes), 8) Podcast talking points. Maintain the core message but adapt tone and format for each platform.

19. Onboarding Flow Designer

You are a UX designer specializing in user onboarding. Design a 5-step onboarding flow for [product]. For each step: describe what the user sees, the action they take, the copy on the screen (headline + body), a progress indicator, and the "aha moment" you're leading them to. Include a skip option strategy and re-engagement email for users who drop off at each step. Target: 80% completion rate.

20. Weekly Report Generator

Act as my executive assistant. Create a professional weekly status report from these notes: [paste your notes/updates]. Format: 1) Executive Summary (3 sentences max), 2) Key Accomplishments (bullet points), 3) Metrics Update (format as table if possible), 4) Challenges & Blockers (with proposed solutions), 5) Next Week Priorities (top 5), 6) Requests/Decisions Needed. Keep the tone professional but concise. Total report should be under 1 page.

21. Hashtag Strategy Generator

Act as a social media growth expert. Create a comprehensive hashtag strategy for [niche/brand] on [platform]. Provide: 10 high-volume hashtags (100K+ posts), 10 medium-volume (10K-100K posts), 10 niche-specific hashtags (under 10K posts), 5 branded hashtag ideas, and 5 trending/seasonal hashtags for this month. For each set, explain the strategy behind the selection and how to rotate them for maximum reach.

22. Product Description Writer

You are an e-commerce copywriter who has written for brands generating $10M+ in sales. Write 3 product descriptions for [product]: 1) Short version (50 words) for catalog/listing, 2) Medium version (150 words) for product page, 3) Long version (300 words) for landing page. Each should highlight benefits over features, include sensory language, address the top objection, and end with a CTA. Target customer: [describe]. Tone: [premium/casual/technical].

23. Interview Question Preparer

Act as a hiring manager for a [role] position at a [company type]. Create 15 interview questions organized into: 5 behavioral questions (STAR format expected), 5 technical/skills questions, 3 culture-fit questions, and 2 situational/problem-solving scenarios. For each question, include: the question, what you're actually assessing, a red flag answer, and a green flag answer. Also include 3 questions the candidate should ask the interviewer.

24. Financial Model Outline

You are a financial analyst. Create a 12-month financial projection for [business type] with these assumptions: [list key assumptions like price, customers, costs]. Include: monthly revenue forecast, COGS, gross margin, operating expenses breakdown (marketing, salaries, tools, etc.), net profit/loss, break-even analysis, and 3 scenarios (conservative, realistic, optimistic). Present as a structured table with monthly columns and explain key assumptions.

25. Process Automation Identifier

Act as a business automation consultant. Here's a description of my weekly workflow: [describe your typical work week, tasks, tools used]. Identify: 1) The top 5 tasks that should be automated immediately (with specific tool recommendations), 2) Tasks that can be partially automated with AI, 3) Tasks that must remain manual and why, 4) Estimated time savings per week for each automation, 5) Step-by-step implementation plan starting with the highest-ROI automation. Tools to consider: Zapier, Make.com, AI assistants, scripts.
